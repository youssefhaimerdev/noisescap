Why background noise works

Your brain is wired to notice change, not constant sound. White noise, brown noise, and nature sounds create an even acoustic blanket that prevents your auditory system from latching onto distractions — leaving your prefrontal cortex free to focus on what matters.

Unlike music with lyrics, pure noise doesn't compete with verbal thinking, making it ideal for reading, writing, coding, and studying. The effect is well-documented in cognitive science: moderate ambient noise (around 70 dB) consistently outperforms silence for creative and analytical tasks.
